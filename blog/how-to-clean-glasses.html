How to Clean Your Glasses the Right Way

Shree Hari Chasma Ghar 22 Feb, 2026 5 min read

Here is a little secret from behind the counter: most scratched lenses are not damaged by accidents — they are worn down by cleaning them the wrong way. Wiping dusty glasses on the corner of your shirt feels harmless, but it is one of the fastest ways to dull your lenses. The good news is that cleaning glasses properly takes less than a minute and keeps them crystal clear for far longer. This simple guide walks you through the safe way to do it.

Why the Wrong Method Scratches Lenses

Modern lenses are protected by thin coatings — anti-glare, scratch-resistant, hydrophobic — that make vision clearer and lenses tougher. But these coatings are not invincible. When you rub a dry, dusty lens with a shirt or tissue, the tiny grit particles sitting on the surface act like sandpaper, dragging fine scratches across the coating. Do that daily and, within months, you are looking through a permanent haze.

The Simple Step-by-Step Method

Cleaning glasses well is mostly about doing things in the right order. Follow these steps:

  1. Wash your hands first. Clean, oil-free hands stop you transferring grease straight back onto the lenses.
  2. Rinse under lukewarm water. Hold the glasses under a gentle stream to float away dust and grit. This is the most important step.
  3. Add a drop of mild soap. Use a tiny amount of gentle dish soap or a proper lens spray on each lens.
  4. Rub gently with fingertips. Softly clean both sides of each lens, the nose pads and the frame with light circular motions.
  5. Rinse again. Wash off all the soap so it does not dry into streaks.
  6. Dry with a clean microfibre cloth. Dab and wipe gently — never scrub.

Quick Tip

Keep two microfibre cloths and rotate them — one in your glasses case, one at home. Wash them by hand every week or two with a little mild soap, and never with fabric softener, which leaves a film that smears your lenses instead of cleaning them.

What to Use — and What to Avoid

The materials you choose make all the difference between clear lenses and scratched ones.

Safe to use:

  • Lukewarm water for rinsing.
  • A drop of mild, lotion-free dish soap or a dedicated lens-cleaning spray.
  • A clean, soft microfibre cloth.

Best avoided:

  • Shirts, tissues, paper towels and napkins — they trap grit and scratch.
  • Household glass cleaners, alcohol, acetone and vinegar — they can strip coatings.
  • Hot water — it may damage certain coatings and lens materials.
  • Saliva or breath-and-wipe — it smears rather than cleans.

Cleaning Glasses On the Go

You will not always be near a tap. For quick touch-ups during the day, carry pre-moistened lens wipes or a small lens spray with a microfibre cloth. If your lenses are dusty and you have nothing to rinse with, it is better to blow off the loose dust and wait than to rub grit across a dry lens. A moment of patience saves a scratch.

Caring for the Whole Pair

Clean lenses are only part of good glasses care. A few extra habits keep the entire pair in shape:

  • Always fold and store glasses in a hard case when not wearing them.
  • Set them down lenses-up, never face-down on a table.
  • Remove and put on glasses with both hands to keep the frame aligned.
  • Keep them away from direct heat, like a car dashboard in summer.

Even with perfect care, glasses need occasional attention. FAQs

Common questions about cleaning glasses

What is the best way to clean glasses?

Rinse the lenses under lukewarm running water to wash away dust, apply a tiny drop of mild dish soap or lens spray, gently rub with clean fingertips, rinse again and dry with a clean microfibre cloth. Rinsing first is the key step, because it removes grit that would otherwise scratch the lens.

Can I use my shirt or a tissue to clean my glasses?

It is best to avoid shirts, tissues and paper towels. They feel soft but contain wood fibres and trapped dust that create tiny scratches over time and can wear down coatings. A clean microfibre cloth is far safer and leaves lenses clearer.

How often should I clean my glasses?

A gentle clean once a day keeps vision sharp and stops oil and dust building up. If you work in dusty conditions or wear makeup, you may want to clean them more often. Always rinse before wiping rather than rubbing dry, dusty lenses.

What should I never use to clean my lenses?

Avoid household glass cleaners, alcohol, acetone, vinegar and hot water, as these can strip or damage lens coatings. Also avoid dry wiping and abrasive cloths. Stick to lukewarm water, mild soap or a proper lens spray, and a microfibre cloth.
