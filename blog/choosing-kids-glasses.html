How to Choose Glasses for Kids

Shree Hari Chasma Ghar 16 Dec, 2025 6 min read

Choosing glasses for a child is a very different task from choosing your own. Kids run, jump, drop things and are not always gentle with their belongings — so a pair that looks lovely on the shelf can be broken or forgotten by the end of the first week. The goal is to find glasses that are tough enough to survive daily life, comfortable enough that your child forgets they are wearing them, and appealing enough that they actually want to keep them on. Here is a parent-friendly guide to getting it right.

Start With an Accurate Eye Test

Everything begins with the right prescription. Children's eyes are still developing, and clear vision is important not just for schoolwork but for their overall growth and confidence. A proper children's eye test measures exactly what your child needs and checks that both eyes are working well together. Never guess a child's power or reuse an old prescription — book a fresh test so the glasses truly help.

Choose a Durable, Flexible Frame

For kids, durability comes first. Look for frames that can take a few knocks and bounce back rather than snap. The best options usually include:

  • Flexible plastic or TR-90 frames that bend and return to shape.
  • Spring hinges on the arms, which flex outward instead of breaking when tugged.
  • Lightweight materials so the glasses feel comfortable all day.
  • Rounded edges and a sturdy build for safety during play.

Quick Tip

Let your child pick the colour or style they like within a set of durable, well-fitting options you have pre-approved. A child who feels they chose their own glasses is far more likely to wear them happily every day.

Get the Fit Just Right

A great frame is useless if it does not fit. Poorly fitting glasses slide down, pinch, or leave the eyes looking out of the wrong part of the lens. When fitting your child's glasses, check that:

  1. The glasses sit level and the eyes are centred in the lenses.
  2. The frame does not slip down the nose when they look down.
  3. The arms rest gently behind the ears without pinching or leaving marks.
  4. Your child can look up, down and side to side comfortably.

For younger children and toddlers, an adjustable strap or cable temples that wrap gently around the ears can keep glasses secure during active play.

Pick the Right Lenses

Lenses matter just as much as frames when it comes to safety and clarity. For children, the standout choice is usually polycarbonate — it is impact-resistant, shatterproof and lightweight, which makes it far safer for an active child. A few worthwhile additions:

  • Scratch-resistant coating to survive plenty of handling.
  • Anti-glare coating for clearer vision in class and on screens.
  • UV protection to shield developing eyes during outdoor play.

Helping Your Child Adjust

Some children slip on their first glasses and never look back; others need a little encouragement. Keep it positive: praise how clearly they can now see the board or their favourite cartoon, start with short wearing sessions if needed, and avoid making it feel like a punishment. Most children adjust within a few days once they realise how much better the world looks. If your child keeps removing their glasses or complaining, mention it to us — it often points to a fit issue that is easy to fix.

Plan for Growth and Spares

Children grow quickly and are hard on their belongings, so it helps to plan ahead. Keep the prescription up to date with regular check-ups, and consider a sturdy case and a spare pair for school if your child depends on their glasses all day. A little planning saves a lot of stress if a pair goes missing or gets bent.

FAQs

Common questions about kids' glasses

What is the best frame material for kids' glasses?

Flexible plastic frames and TR-90 or similar bendable materials are ideal for children because they bounce back instead of snapping. Frames with spring hinges also survive rough handling well. These materials are light, comfortable and stand up to the drops and knocks of daily play.

Which lenses are best for children?

Polycarbonate lenses are usually the best choice for kids because they are impact-resistant, lightweight and shatterproof, which makes them safer for active children. An anti-glare and scratch-resistant coating helps them stay clear, and UV protection guards young eyes outdoors.

How do I make sure my child's glasses fit correctly?

The glasses should sit level, with the eyes centred in the lenses. The frame should not slide down the nose or pinch behind the ears, and the arms should hold gently without leaving marks. A good fitting at the optician makes a huge difference to comfort and how happily your child wears them.

How can I help my child get used to wearing glasses?

Let your child help choose a frame they like so they feel involved, start with short, positive wearing sessions, and offer plenty of encouragement. Most children adapt within a few days once they notice how much clearer they can see. A comfortable, well-fitted pair makes the process much easier.
