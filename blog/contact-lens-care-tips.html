Contact Lens Care and Hygiene Tips

Shree Hari Chasma Ghar 16 Jan, 2026 6 min read

Contact lenses offer wonderful freedom — clear vision without frames, no fogging, and no glasses slipping down your nose. But because they sit directly on your eye, they demand a little more responsibility than glasses do. The vast majority of contact lens problems come down to one thing: hygiene. The good news is that safe lens wear is simple once it becomes routine. Follow these contact lens care tips and you can enjoy comfortable, worry-free vision every day.

Start With Clean Hands, Every Time

Your hands are the bridge between your lenses and your eyes, so they must be spotless. Before touching your lenses, wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water, then dry them on a clean, lint-free towel. Avoid oily or heavily fragranced soaps, which can leave residue on the lens surface. This single habit prevents a large share of eye irritations.

The Right Way to Clean and Store Lenses

Cleaning is not just about rinsing — a gentle rub matters. Here is the reliable routine:

  1. Place the lens in your clean palm and add a few drops of fresh solution.
  2. Rub the lens gently with a fingertip for a few seconds to loosen deposits.
  3. Rinse it with more fresh solution.
  4. Store it in a clean case filled with fresh solution — never yesterday's leftover solution.

Always use the solution recommended for your lens type, and never top up old solution with new. "Topping up" dilutes the disinfectant and lets bacteria survive.

Never Let Water Near Your Lenses

This rule is worth repeating because it is so important. Tap water, bottled water, saliva and swimming pool water can all carry microbes that stick to lenses and cause serious infections. Keep the following in mind:

  • Do not rinse or store lenses in tap water — only in proper solution.
  • Never wet a lens with saliva, however tempting when out and about.
  • Remove lenses before swimming or showering, or wear tight goggles.
  • Keep lenses out during activities where splashing is likely.

Safety First

If your eye becomes red, painful, watery or sensitive to light, remove your lenses immediately and do not wear them again until the problem clears. If symptoms persist or worsen, see an eye care professional promptly — never push through discomfort with lenses in.

Respect the Wearing Schedule

Lenses are designed to be worn and replaced on a set schedule — daily, fortnightly or monthly. Wearing them longer than intended is a common and risky shortcut. Old lenses accumulate deposits, become less breathable and greatly increase the chance of irritation and infection. Daily disposables are thrown away each night; reusable lenses must be cleaned and stored correctly and replaced on time. Mark your replacement date so you never lose track.

Look After Your Lens Case

People carefully clean their lenses and then store them in a neglected case — which defeats the purpose. Your case needs care too. Rinse it with fresh solution after each use, wipe it and let it air-dry face down on a clean tissue. Replace the case every one to three months. A grimy case can harbour a biofilm of bacteria that recontaminates even perfectly clean lenses.

Everyday Habits for Comfort

A few extra habits keep lens wear pleasant day to day:

  • Put lenses in before applying make-up and remove them before taking make-up off.
  • Use lubricating drops made for contact lenses if your eyes feel dry.
  • Give your eyes a break with glasses on long, tiring or unwell days.
  • Keep a backup pair of glasses so you are never forced to over-wear lenses.

Wear Lenses With Confidence

Contact lenses are safe and comfortable when treated with a little daily care — clean hands, fresh solution, no water contact, and sticking to your replacement schedule. FAQs

Common questions about contact lens care

How should I clean my contact lenses?

Always wash and dry your hands first, then place the lens in your palm, add fresh solution and rub gently for a few seconds before rinsing. Never use tap water or saliva to clean lenses, and always use fresh solution rather than topping up old solution.

Can I sleep while wearing contact lenses?

Unless your lenses are specifically approved for overnight wear, you should remove them before sleeping. Sleeping in ordinary lenses reduces oxygen to the eye and significantly raises the risk of irritation and infection.

How often should I replace my contact lens case?

Replace your lens case at least every one to three months, and rinse it with fresh solution and air-dry it after each use. A dirty or old case can harbour bacteria that put your eyes at risk even if the lenses themselves are clean.

Can I wear contact lenses while swimming or showering?

It is best to avoid water contact with lenses. Water in pools, showers and taps can carry germs that stick to lenses and cause serious eye infections. If you must swim, use tight goggles or choose daily disposables you can discard afterwards.
