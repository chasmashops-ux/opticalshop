Contact Lenses: A Beginner's Guide

Shree Hari Chasma Ghar 20 Jan, 2026 7 min read

Contact lenses can feel like a wonderful upgrade — clear vision with nothing on your face, freedom for sports, and the confidence to go frame-free when you want to. But if you have never worn them, the idea of putting something on your eye can be a little nerve-wracking. Take a breath: millions of people wear lenses comfortably every day, and with the right guidance you will too. This beginner's guide walks you through the types, the safety basics and exactly what to expect when you start.

Why People Switch to Contact Lenses

Contacts are not about replacing your glasses entirely — most wearers keep both and choose whichever suits the moment. People love lenses because they offer:

  • A natural field of view with no frame edges or reflections.
  • Freedom for activities like sports, dancing and workouts.
  • No fogging or slipping in the rain, heat or while wearing a mask.
  • A confidence boost for occasions when you would rather not wear frames.

The Main Types of Contact Lenses

Before you start, it helps to know what is available. Your optician will recommend the best match for your eyes and routine:

  • Daily disposables: a fresh pair each day, thrown away at night. The most hygienic and low-maintenance option, ideal for beginners.
  • Monthly lenses: worn during the day and stored in solution overnight, replaced every month. More economical for regular wearers.
  • Toric lenses: specially shaped for people with astigmatism.
  • Coloured lenses: to enhance or change your eye colour, available with or without power.

Quick Tip

For your very first lenses, daily disposables are the easiest and safest choice. There is no cleaning or storage to remember, and a fresh, sterile lens every morning greatly lowers the risk of irritation while you learn.

Start With a Proper Fitting

This is the step you must not skip. Contact lenses are medical devices that sit directly on your eye, so they need to match not just your power but also the curve and size of your eye. A proper eye test and fitting make sure your lenses are comfortable and safe — never buy lenses off the shelf based on your glasses prescription alone. During the fitting, your optician will also teach you how to insert, remove and care for them.

How to Put In and Take Out Your Lenses

It feels tricky on day one and easy by day three. Here is the basic method:

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and dry them on a lint-free towel.
  2. Place the lens on your fingertip and check it forms a smooth bowl, not a flared saucer, so it is not inside out.
  3. Hold your upper eyelid open, pull the lower lid down, and look up or straight ahead.
  4. Gently rest the lens on your eye, then blink slowly to settle it in place.
  5. To remove, look up, pull down the lower lid, and gently pinch the lens out with clean fingers.

Go slowly and stay relaxed. If a lens feels uncomfortable, take it out, rinse it with fresh solution, and try again.

Safety and Hygiene Rules That Matter

Contacts are safe when you respect a few golden rules. Ignoring them is the main cause of eye infections, so treat these as non-negotiable:

  • Always wash and dry your hands before touching your lenses.
  • Never sleep in lenses unless they are specifically approved for it.
  • Never top up old solution — use fresh solution every time for monthly lenses.
  • Keep lenses away from water; do not swim or shower in them without protection.
  • Stick to the replacement schedule and never over-wear a lens to save money.
  • Remove your lenses and see a professional if your eyes become red, painful or blurry.

What to Expect in the First Few Weeks

Your eyes need a little time to get used to lenses. Most beginners start with just a few hours a day and build up gradually. Some mild awareness of the lens is normal at first and usually fades quickly. Within a week or two, most people forget they are even wearing them. If discomfort persists rather than settles, that is a sign to go back for a check rather than push through.

Ready to Try Contacts?

Contact lenses open up a lot of everyday freedom, and starting the right way makes all the difference. Get a proper fitting, choose beginner-friendly daily lenses, and follow the hygiene rules — do that, and your first experience should be smooth and comfortable.

FAQs

Common questions about starting contact lenses

Are contact lenses safe for beginners?

Yes, contact lenses are safe for most people when fitted properly and used with good hygiene. The key is to start with a proper eye test and fitting, follow the wearing schedule your optician gives you, and always keep your hands and lenses clean. Never use lenses without professional guidance.

How do I put in a contact lens for the first time?

Wash and dry your hands, place the lens on the tip of your index finger and check it is not inside out. Hold your upper lid with one hand, pull down the lower lid with the other, look up or ahead, and gently place the lens on your eye. Blink slowly and it will settle into place. Take it slowly at first.

How long can I wear contact lenses each day?

Most beginners start with a few hours a day and gradually build up as their eyes adjust. Many people comfortably wear lenses for eight to twelve hours, but you should follow the limit your optician sets and remove them if your eyes feel dry, red or irritated. Always take them out before sleeping unless told otherwise.

Can I wear contact lenses if I have astigmatism or high power?

Usually yes. There are special toric lenses for astigmatism and a wide range of powers available. The right choice depends on your prescription and eye shape, which is why an eye test and fitting are essential before you buy. We can advise which lenses suit your eyes best.
