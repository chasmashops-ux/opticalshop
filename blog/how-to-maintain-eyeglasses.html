How to Maintain Your Eyeglasses

Shree Hari Chasma Ghar 18 Feb, 2026 5 min read

A good pair of glasses is an investment you rely on from the moment you wake up. Yet most of us treat them roughly — pushing them up with a fingertip, wiping them on a shirt, tossing them lens-down on the desk. Over months, those small habits add up to scratched lenses, loose arms and frames that never sit quite right. The happy news is that keeping glasses in great shape takes only a few seconds a day and a handful of easy habits. Here is everything you need to know to make your eyewear last.

Clean Your Lenses the Right Way

Smudged lenses are annoying, but cleaning them badly is what causes lasting damage. Dust and grit act like sandpaper if you rub them across a dry lens. Follow this simple routine:

  1. Rinse the lenses under lukewarm running water to wash away dust and grit.
  2. Add a tiny drop of mild dish soap or use a proper lens spray.
  3. Gently rub both sides with your fingertips, including the edges and nose pads.
  4. Rinse again and dry with a clean, dry microfibre cloth.

Never use tissue, paper towel, your shirt or your dupatta on dry lenses — they trap grit and leave fine scratches that build up over time.

Quick Tip

Keep two microfibre cloths — one at home and one in your bag — and wash them every couple of weeks with plain water. A dirty cloth just smears old grime and oil back onto your lenses, so a clean cloth is half the secret to spotless glasses.

Handle Them With Both Hands

Here is a habit almost everyone gets wrong. Taking your glasses off with one hand tugs on a single arm, and over time that twists the frame out of alignment and loosens the hinge. Always use both hands to lift the glasses evenly off both ears. It feels like a small thing, but it is one of the biggest reasons frames go crooked.

Store Them Properly

Where you put your glasses when you are not wearing them matters just as much as how you clean them. A few rules keep both lenses and frame safe:

  • Use a hard case whenever the glasses go into a bag or pocket.
  • Never rest them lens-down on any surface — set them folded with lenses facing up if a case is not handy.
  • Keep them off the car dashboard, where Ahmedabad heat can warp the frame and damage coatings.
  • Don't perch them on your head; this stretches the arms and loosens the fit.

Protect Them From Heat and Chemicals

Lens coatings and frame materials do not enjoy extreme conditions. Leaving glasses in a hot car, near a stove, or in direct summer sun can craze the coating and soften plastic frames. Household chemicals, hairspray, perfume and cleaning sprays can also eat into coatings, so take your glasses off before using them and keep them away from strong cleaners.

Keep the Fit Comfortable

Glasses that slide down or pinch are not just annoying — a poor fit means you are constantly touching and adjusting them, which wears them out faster. Watch for these signs that your frames need attention:

  • They slip down your nose during the day.
  • One lens sits higher than the other.
  • The arms feel too tight or leave marks behind your ears.
  • The frame wobbles or twists when you put it on.

Resist the urge to bend the frame yourself. A quick professional adjustment is usually free and gets the alignment exactly right without risking a snapped arm.

A Little Care Goes a Long Way

None of this takes real effort — clean gently once a day, use both hands, store them in a case, and keep them away from heat and chemicals. Do that, and your glasses will stay clear, comfortable and good-looking for years rather than months, saving you money and hassle along the way.

FAQs

Common questions about caring for glasses

How often should I clean my glasses?

A quick clean once a day keeps lenses clear and comfortable, and you can rinse them more often if they get smudged or dusty. Rinse with water first, add a drop of mild soap or lens spray, then dry with a clean microfibre cloth. Regular gentle cleaning prevents grime from building up and scratching the surface.

Why should I use both hands to remove my glasses?

Taking your glasses off with one hand puts uneven pressure on one arm, which slowly loosens the hinge and bends the frame out of shape. Using both hands to lift them evenly off your ears keeps the fit correct and stops the frame from becoming crooked over time.

Is it bad to leave glasses lens-down on a table?

Yes. Resting glasses lens-down lets the lenses rub against the surface and pick up scratches, which no cleaning can remove. Always fold them and place them in a hard case, or set them down folded and resting on the arms with the lenses facing up.

Can I get my glasses adjusted for free?

Most opticians, including us, are happy to realign and tighten your frames. If your glasses feel loose, sit crooked or pinch, bring them in for a quick adjustment rather than bending them yourself. A professional tune-up keeps them comfortable and lasting longer.
