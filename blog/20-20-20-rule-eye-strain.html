The 20-20-20 Rule for Eye Strain

Shree Hari Chasma Ghar 14 May, 2026 5 min read

Most of us now spend hours a day staring at a screen — laptops for work, phones for messages, tablets for entertainment. By evening, your eyes can feel dry, heavy and tired, and you may notice a dull ache behind them or a blurry moment when you look up. This is digital eye strain, and it is one of the most common complaints we hear. The good news is that a remarkably simple habit can help: the 20-20-20 rule. It costs nothing, takes seconds, and fits into the busiest day.

What Is the 20-20-20 Rule?

The rule is easy to remember: every 20 minutes, look at something about 20 feet (roughly 6 metres) away for at least 20 seconds. That is the whole idea. You do not stop working, you do not need any equipment — you simply lift your gaze from the screen and let your eyes rest on something in the distance for a short moment.

Those few seconds give the tiny focusing muscles inside your eyes a chance to relax, and they remind you to blink properly, which most people forget to do while concentrating on a screen.

Why Screens Tire Your Eyes

When you look at anything close up, the muscles inside your eyes contract to keep the image sharp. Hold that focus for hours and, like any muscle, they get fatigued. Screens add two more problems on top of this:

  • You blink far less. Concentration cuts your blink rate, so your eyes dry out.
  • The focusing distance never changes. Your eyes stay locked on one close plane with no relief.

The 20-20-20 rule tackles both at once — the distant glance relaxes the focusing muscles, and the natural blink that comes with it re-wets the surface of your eyes.

Quick Tip

Pair the rule with a full, slow blink each time you look away. During intense screen work people often blink only a third as much as normal, which is a big cause of that gritty, dry feeling by evening.

How to Make the Habit Stick

The hardest part is not the rule itself — it is remembering to do it. Try these ideas:

  1. Set a soft timer or reminder every 20 minutes on your phone or computer.
  2. Tie the break to a natural pause, like finishing an email, saving a file or ending a call.
  3. Keep a window, a wall clock or a plant in view so there is always something distant to look at.
  4. Use a sticky note on your monitor as a gentle prompt for the first few days.
  5. Stand up occasionally as well — a short stretch helps your whole body, not just your eyes.

More Simple Ways to Ease Eye Strain

The 20-20-20 rule works best alongside a few other easy habits:

  • Adjust your screen: keep it about an arm's length away and slightly below eye level.
  • Fix the lighting: avoid harsh glare on the screen and do not work in a completely dark room.
  • Use comfortable text size: if you are leaning in to read, make the font bigger.
  • Stay hydrated: drinking enough water supports your tear film, especially in dry, air-conditioned rooms.
  • Consider blue cut or anti-glare lenses: if you spend all day on screens, they can add real comfort.

When Eye Strain Needs More Than a Break

The 20-20-20 rule is a wonderful daily habit, but it is not a cure for every eye problem. If your discomfort keeps coming back, pay attention to warning signs such as:

  • Frequent headaches, especially around the forehead or temples.
  • Blurred vision that does not clear after resting.
  • Needing to squint or hold things closer to see them.
  • Aching or watering eyes even on lighter screen days.

These can be a sign that you need an updated prescription or lenses better suited to screen work. A simple eye test can find out — and often the fix is easier than you expect.

A Small Habit With a Big Payoff

Your eyes work hard for you every waking hour, and screens ask a lot of them. The beauty of the 20-20-20 rule is that it asks so little in return: a few seconds, a few times an hour, and your eyes stay fresher, longer. Build it into your routine this week and you will likely feel the difference by the end of the day.

FAQs

Common questions about the 20-20-20 rule

What is the 20-20-20 rule?

The 20-20-20 rule is a simple habit for screen users: every 20 minutes, look at something about 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds. This short break relaxes the focusing muscles inside your eyes and reminds you to blink, which helps prevent tired, dry and strained eyes.

Does the 20-20-20 rule really work?

It is a widely recommended habit because it gives your eyes regular short rests from close-up focusing. It will not cure an underlying vision problem, but for everyday digital eye strain it is a gentle, effective way to keep your eyes more comfortable through the day.

How can I remember to take these breaks?

Set a gentle timer or reminder on your phone or computer every 20 minutes, or tie the break to natural pauses like finishing an email or a call. Many people also keep a plant or window in view so there is always something distant to glance at.

When should I see an optician about eye strain?

If eye strain comes with headaches, blurred vision, or discomfort that does not ease after breaks and rest, it is worth getting an eye test. You may need an updated prescription or lenses suited to screen work. A quick check can rule out other issues too.
